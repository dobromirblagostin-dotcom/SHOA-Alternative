# Baalbek: who moved the megaliths that we cannot move today?

In the ruins of Baalbek (Lebanon) lies a stone block known as the "Stone of the Pregnant Woman." It weighs approximately 1,000 tons. Nearby, in a quarry, lies an even larger block — around 1,650 tons. It was never moved.

Official history tells us these stones were cut and transported by the Romans. But here is the question that refuses to go away:

How?

Modern cranes cannot lift 1,650 tons in one piece. The largest mobile crane today, the Liebherr LR 13000, has a maximum capacity of 3,000 tons — but it requires a fully assembled boom, a prepared site, and a team of engineers. The Romans supposedly achieved this with wooden rollers, ropes, and manual labor.

Is this a plausible explanation — or a placeholder for something we do not understand?

Andrey Sklyarov and his LAI team studied Baalbek extensively. Maxim Gaubets filmed it. The question they left us with is not whether the Romans could have done it. It is whether anyone, with any technology known to us today, could replicate it.
