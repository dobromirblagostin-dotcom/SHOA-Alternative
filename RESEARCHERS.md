# Researchers

This file lists the key researchers and projects in the field of alternative history whose work inspires and informs SHOA ALTERNATIVE. We do not claim that their conclusions are final or correct. We honor them for asking the right questions.

---

## In Memoriam

**Andrey Sklyarov** (1961–2016)
- **Project:** Laboratory of Alternative History (LAI)
- **Website:** [lah.ru](https://lah.ru)
- **Focus:** Paleocontact hypothesis, stone processing technologies on ancient megaliths, analysis of structures that cannot be replicated with modern technology. Sklyarov conducted numerous expeditions to Egypt, Peru, Mexico, Easter Island, and other sites. His work remains foundational for the entire field.

## Contemporary Researchers

**Maxim Gaubets**
- **Project:** Forgotten Ruins (Забытые руины)
- **Website:** [ruins.su](https://ruins.su)
- **Focus:** Polygonal masonry, giant stone blocks, "buried" floors of buildings, ancient sarcophagi, underground channels. Active field researcher with expeditions in Turkey, the Middle East, and Russia.

**Ilya Gerasimov** ("SNT Chairman")
- **Channel:** Председатель СНТ (Telegram, YouTube)
- **Focus:** Inconsistencies in official history, the phenomenon of "buried" first floors of historical buildings (estates, churches, urban houses), alternative explanations for large-scale historical falsifications.

**Oleg Pavlyuchenko**
- **Project:** AISPIK (Alternative History of the Northern Black Sea and Caucasus)
- **Website:** [aispik.ru](https://aispik.ru)
- **Focus:** "Forensic method" in alternative history — searching for material and informational traces to reconstruct the past. Regional focus on the Northern Black Sea, Caucasus, Turkey, Sri Lanka, UAE, North Africa.

**Alexey Kungurov**
- **Focus:** Theory of a global catastrophe in the 19th century (possibly nuclear), which destroyed a significant part of civilization and led to the loss of knowledge and technology. Analysis of dating inconsistencies, artifacts, and historical events from this perspective.

## Note

All these researchers emphasize that their goal is not to prove a single dogma, but to expose inconsistencies in the official version and stimulate critical thinking. Their methods and conclusions are often criticized by professional historians and archaeologists.

We include them here not as authorities, but as voices that dared to ask forbidden questions.
